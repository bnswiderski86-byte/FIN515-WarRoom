Opening
Good [morning/afternoon], Professor Appleyard and classmates. My name is [NAME], and I am speaking on behalf of Pandora A/S — the world's largest jewelry brand by volume, and a company that made a documented strategic decision in 2021: that the future of diamonds is not found underground.
Opening
The question before us is specific. Under the SCS-007 Jewelry Sustainability Standard — five pillars evaluating verified traceability, ethical stewardship, climate neutrality, sustainable production, and sustainability investment — which production model presents the stronger path to certification? Our answer is Pandora. Here is why.
Pillar 1 — Verified Origin Traceability
Opening
SCS-007 requires greater than 99% origin accuracy, blockchain documentation, and certified handlers at every custody transfer. The mined diamond challenge is structural — a stone pulled from the earth has passed through geological time before any human documentation begins. De Beers verifies provenance across four countries, 300,000 people, 77 countries downstream.

Pandora's CVD diamonds originate in a controlled growth chamber. We know the origin of every single diamond. Our Responsible Sourcing Programme is OECD-aligned. All precious metals from 100% RJC Chain of Custody-certified refiners. We have a blockchain gap — we acknowledge it — but we are closing a smaller structural gap from a stronger starting position. Our primary research confirmed this directly: a Pandora store associate confirmed every lab-grown diamond includes documentation of how it was made and where it was grown.
Pillar 3 — Climate Neutrality [Structural Advantage]
Opening
Pandora's lab-grown diamonds carry a carbon footprint of approximately 11.4 kg CO₂e per one-carat gold ring — roughly 95% lower than the mined equivalent. This is not marketing — it is a Carbon Trust-verified lifecycle assessment.

Since 2024, Pandora's operations run on 100% renewable electricity. Our SBTi Scope 1+2 target was surpassed ahead of schedule. In May 2024, Pandora issued a €500M sustainability-linked bond where the interest rate is tied to 2028 emissions targets — financial accountability, not a pledge.

De Beers committed to a 42% Scope 1+2 reduction by 2030. As of 2024, they've achieved approximately 7% — from their own self-reported data. More critically, SCS-007 uniquely requires legacy emissions accounting — the carbon from historical operations. De Beers has been mining since 1888. That 130-year burden cannot be offset.
Pillar 4 — Sustainable Production
Opening
SCS-007 requires net-zero impacts in ≥50% of life cycle impact categories. Pandora's CVD production uses 100% recycled silver & gold, 100% renewable electricity. No land disturbance. No tailings. No ecosystem destruction. Mining, by nature, displaces earth, consumes water, generates tailings — documented in De Beers' own 2024 sustainability report.
Framing — Honest About Our Gaps
Opening
We will be direct. Pandora has two weak pillars — Ethical Stewardship (2018–19 labor violation, 92 unlawful dismissals in Thailand) and Sustainability Investment (no ASM community investment documented). We are not hiding these.

But the microeconomic reality: SCS-007 evaluates the full social cost of diamond production — including externalities mining imposes on communities, climate, and ecosystems. Under that lens, the lab-grown model is the structurally more certifiable path. Not because Pandora is perfect — because we have already eliminated the negative externalities De Beers can never fully remediate. Thank you.

Pandora is not yet certified. Neither is De Beers. But of the two, Pandora has already structurally solved the problems that matter most. Judge not who has been sustainable longer — but who is more certifiable, today and tomorrow, under SCS-007. Thank you.
